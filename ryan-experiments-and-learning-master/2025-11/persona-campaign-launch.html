Building the Foundation: Personas & Campaign Strategy

How we created a systematic approach to healthcare transparency marketing grounded in real pain points and PBGH-validated data

PS
Payerset Team • 8 min read

Today marks a significant milestone. We've built the foundation for how we'll communicate healthcare transparency to the people who need it most—patients crushed by surprise bills, employers overpaying by millions, and providers fighting an unfair system.

This isn't just marketing. It's the architecture for opening eyes to problems people don't know they have.

The Persona Framework

We started with a fundamental question: Who are we trying to help, and what keeps them up at night?

The answer required deep research into three distinct audiences, each trapped in the same broken system but experiencing it differently:

Sarah: The Surprise Bill Victim

She's 34, works in marketing, has a family of four, and just got a $2,400 medical bill after an ER visit. She thought insurance would cover more. She stares at the bill for weeks, unsure what to do. She doesn't know:

  • That 80% of medical bills contain errors
  • That she has rights under the No Surprises Act
  • That cash prices are often cheaper than her "negotiated" insurance rate
  • That she can actually negotiate

The core insight: Sarah thinks the system is working as designed and she just got unlucky. She doesn't realize the system is designed to overcharge and that she has power to fight back.

Jennifer: The Overwhelmed Benefits Manager

She's 38, manages healthcare benefits for 800 employees, and oversees an $8-25M annual spend. Her costs keep rising 8-12% every year. Her broker shows "great discount reports." Employees complain. She has no good answers. She doesn't know:

  • She's probably overpaying 20-30% (PBGH proved this)
  • "Discounts" are off meaningless chargemaster prices
  • Her broker has conflicts of interest (commission structure)
  • Spread pricing might be costing her millions
  • She has fiduciary duty with personal liability

The core insight: Jennifer thinks she's doing her job. She doesn't realize the system is designed to extract money from her company while keeping her in the dark. She's not failing—she's being deliberately misled.

Dr. Patel: The Independent Physician Group

He's 52, runs an independent multi-specialty clinic with 12 physicians. They deliver Grade A care with 96% patient satisfaction. But hospital-employed doctors get paid 40% more for the same services. His volume is declining. He doesn't know:

  • Employers would direct contract if they knew he existed
  • His rates are often below cost (underpaid by $300K-500K annually)
  • Quality data exists that proves his value
  • Direct contracting could add $600K annual revenue
  • Transparency regulations actually work in his favor

The core insight: Dr. Patel thinks he's losing patients because he's worse. He doesn't realize patients are being steered through opaque insurance design. His clinical excellence is a competitive advantage only if people can see it.

Pattern Recognition: Each persona is trapped by the same dynamic—information asymmetry. They don't know what they don't know, and the system profits from that ignorance.

The Three-Stage Educational Journey

Once we understood the personas, we mapped their transformation:

  1. Stage 1: Problem Awareness - Help them realize they have a problem they didn't know existed
  2. Stage 2: Understanding & Empowerment - Teach them they have power and tools to act
  3. Stage 3: Readiness for Solution - Create demand for Payerset products before launch

Today, we completed Stage 1.

For Patients: The Billing Truth

"Why Your Medical Bill Is Probably Wrong" reveals the 80% error rate and gives permission to question. "The Expensive Hospital Has Worse Safety Scores" shows price doesn't equal quality using Leapfrog data. "Real Stories: People Who Cut Their Bills in Half" provides proof that fighting back works.

Every piece addresses a specific awareness gap and provides a concrete call-to-action—download a checklist, use a comparison tool, share with someone who needs it.

For Employers: The PBGH Revelation

"The PBGH Revelation" documents the uncomfortable boardroom moments when employers discovered 30% overpayment. "5 Signs Your Benefits Consultant Has Conflicts of Interest" provides a diagnostic tool. "What Your Broker Won't Tell You About Spread Pricing" exposes the hidden fees with a calculator.

Each piece is designed to create the moment where a CFO or benefits manager can no longer pretend everything is fine.

For Providers: The Transparency Opportunity

"Why Independent Practices Get Paid Less for Better Care" validates their frustration with data. "Employers Are Looking for You" shows the direct contracting opportunity with real numbers ($600K additional revenue). "The Transparency Opportunity" provides a step-by-step playbook.

The message: Your quality is your competitive advantage, but only if the market can see it.

Grounded in Real Data

Every claim in every piece is backed by verifiable data:

"PBGH found employers paying 30% more than peers for identical care. That's not a guess. That's documented, peer-reviewed research from the Purchaser Business Group on Health's Transparency Demonstration Project."
  • 80% of medical bills contain errors - Cited, verifiable
  • Zero correlation between price and quality - PBGH finding
  • Direct contracts beat TPA rates by 15-25% - PBGH data
  • Hospital-employed physicians paid 40-80% more - CMS research
  • $600K spread pricing on single infusion patient - Real case from transparency data

We're not selling hope. We're presenting evidence.

What We Built

The campaign includes production-ready content with multiple formats for each piece:

  • Blog articles (800-1,500 words each)
  • Social media carousels (LinkedIn, Instagram)
  • Twitter threads (8-10 tweets, ready to post)
  • Interactive tools (calculators, quizzes, comparison tools)
  • Video concepts (testimonials, explainers)
  • Downloadable resources (checklists, guides, templates)

Plus comprehensive measurement frameworks:

  • Awareness metrics (reach, engagement, time on page)
  • Lead capture (email signups, downloads, tool usage)
  • Problem recognition (quiz results, self-assessments)
  • Engagement depth (multiple pieces consumed, sharing behavior)

Why This Matters

Healthcare transparency isn't just a business opportunity. It's a correction to a fundamentally broken market.

When patients don't know they're being overcharged, they pay.

When employers don't know they're overpaying by 30%, they keep writing checks.

When providers can't showcase their quality, the market rewards branding over outcomes.

Information asymmetry is the disease. Transparency is the cure.

These personas aren't abstractions. They're real people:

  • The woman putting medical bills on a credit card
  • The benefits manager who can't explain why costs keep rising
  • The independent physician watching volume decline despite excellent care

Every content piece we create has one purpose: help them see what they couldn't see before.

Once they see it, they can't unsee it.

And that's when change happens.

"You can't fix what you can't see." — Cora Opsahl

Today, we built the tools to help people see.

Tomorrow, we help them fix it.
