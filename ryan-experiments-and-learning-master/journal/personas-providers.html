Meet Dr. Patel: The Independent Physician Group

52, Partner, Independent Multi-Specialty Clinic (12 physicians)

12 physicians + 30 staff Primary care + specialists Grade A outcomes Independent (not hospital-owned)

Current Pain Points: What Dr. Patel Experiences

Rate Disparity

Hospital-employed doctors get paid 40% more for the exact same services. Same procedure, same quality—but unfair reimbursement.

Volume Declining

Patients are steered to the hospital system through narrow insurance networks. Despite better care, he's losing patients.

Quality Ignored

Practice has Grade A outcomes and high patient satisfaction. But employers don't know. Quality data exists but isn't surfaced.

Administrative Burden

Employs 1 biller per 2 doctors. Constantly fighting claim denials. Spending more time on billing than patient care.

No Negotiating Power

Insurance carriers say "accept our rates or be out of network." Feels trapped between bad rates and losing all patients.

Existential Threat

Private equity firms circling, offering buyouts. Practice worth more sold than continuing independently. Younger partners are tempted.

The Awareness Gap: What Dr. Patel Doesn't Know

Dr. Patel's clinical excellence could be his competitive advantage—if only the market could see it.

❌
Employers would direct contract if they knew — Thinks employers are locked into TPAs and carrier networks
❌
His rates are often below cost — Doesn't have data to prove he's being underpaid by $300K-$500K annually
❌
Quality data exists and proves his value — Doesn't know how to access or market with Leapfrog and outcomes data
❌
Direct contracting market is growing — Thinks it's too complex or risky, never considered it viable
❌
He has leverage with data — Feels powerless against large hospital systems, doesn't realize transparency changes dynamics
❌
Transparency regulations work in his favor — Sees CAA compliance as burden, not opportunity to showcase value
❌
Cash-pay could be viable revenue stream — Never marketed transparent pricing to cash patients
❌
Patients are being steered, not choosing — Thinks patients prefer hospitals, doesn't see opaque insurance design
The Core Insight

Dr. Patel thinks he's losing patients because he's worse. He doesn't realize patients are being steered through opaque insurance design, and that transparency can shift market dynamics in his favor. His clinical excellence is a competitive advantage only if people can see it.
