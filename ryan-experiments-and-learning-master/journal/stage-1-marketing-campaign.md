# Stage 1 Marketing Campaign: Problem Awareness
## "You're Being Overcharged (And You Didn't Even Know It)"

**Campaign Duration:** Months 1-2
**Goal:** Help each persona realize they have a problem they didn't know existed
**Success Metric:** 30% of visitors identify with at least one pain point and take next action (email signup, share content, or explore further)

---

## Campaign Philosophy

**The Core Strategy:**
People don't seek solutions to problems they don't know exist. This campaign reveals hidden problems through:
1. **Shocking revelations** - Data that contradicts what they've been told
2. **Relatable stories** - Real people experiencing real pain
3. **Permission to question** - "It's not your fault, but now you know"
4. **Proof points** - PBGH study, real numbers, verifiable data

**What We're NOT Doing:**
- Selling products (not yet)
- Being preachy or judgmental
- Overwhelming with complexity
- Making them feel stupid

**What We ARE Doing:**
- Opening eyes to hidden realities
- Creating "I'm not alone" moments
- Building trust through transparency
- Establishing authority through data

### Content Piece #3: "What Your Broker Won't Tell You About Spread Pricing"
**Format:** Investigative article (1,200 words) + infographic + calculator tool
**Distribution:** CFO/finance publications, LinkedIn, benefits forums, direct email

**Hook:**
> **Employer pays:** $28,000 for infusion therapy
> **Provider receives:** $12,000 for same infusion
> **Difference:** $16,000
>
> **Where did $16,000 go?**
>
> Your TPA took it. They call it "spread pricing." You call it theft.
>
> **For a $50M healthcare spend, typical spread = $2.5M-$5M annually.**
>
> **Your broker knows. They just hoped you wouldn't find out.**

**Structure:**

**Part 1: What Is Spread Pricing?**
- Simple definition with visual diagram
- How it works (TPA contracts with provider at $X, charges employer $Y, keeps $Y-$X)
- Why it's invisible (aggregated reporting, no line-item disclosure)
- Legal gray area (not illegal, but ethically dubious)

**Part 2: Real Examples**
- Infusion therapy: $1.2M charged to employer ‚Üí $600K paid to provider = $600K spread
- Imaging services: 200-400% spreads common
- Specialty medications: PBM spreads of 600%+ documented
- Site-of-service: Same MRI, $3,000 hospital vs. $400 independent = $2,600 spread (all to TPA)

**Part 3: How They Hide It**
- No line-item disclosure in reports
- "Administrative fees" language in contracts
- "Proprietary pricing" claims
- Aggregated reporting (can't see individual claims)
- Contractual barriers to auditing
- Threatening legal action if you investigate

**Part 4: How to Detect It**
- Request data: In-network file + remittance amounts
- Compare: What TPA says they negotiated vs. what provider actually received
- Analyze: Calculate spread percentage by procedure/provider
- Document: Build audit trail for TPA confrontation

**Part 5: What to Do About It**
- Demand transparency clause in contract
- Require pass-through pricing (TPA makes $0 on spread)
- Audit rights in contract
- Switch to unconflicted TPA
- Direct contracting (cut out middleman entirely)

**Interactive Tool: "Spread Pricing Calculator"**

User inputs:
- Annual healthcare spend
- TPA/carrier name
- Access to claims data (yes/no)

Tool estimates:
- Likely spread percentage (based on industry data)
- Estimated annual leakage
- How much could be saved with pass-through pricing
- ROI of transparency audit
