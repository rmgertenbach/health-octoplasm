Meet Sarah: The Surprise Bill Victim

34-year-old Marketing Manager, Family of 4

$60K-$120K household income High-deductible health plan Suburban/urban professional Moderate tech-savvy

Current Pain Points: What Sarah Experiences

Shock and Confusion

Got a medical bill for $2,400 after an ER visit. Thought insurance would cover more. The number feels arbitrary and terrifying.

Paralysis

Doesn't know if the bill is correct, fair, or if she can even negotiate. Stares at the bill for weeks, unsure what to do.

Financial Stress

Bill goes on a credit card, accrues interest, affects credit score. The emergency became a financial emergency.

Guilt and Anger

"I did everything right—have insurance, went to an in-network hospital. Why am I still stuck with this massive bill?"

Complexity Overwhelm

The EOB is incomprehensible. Provider billing office is unhelpful. Insurance says "it's your deductible." Nobody explains anything.

Trust Erosion

Feels scammed by the system. Now avoids care in the future, even when she needs it. Fear of bills > fear of illness.

The Awareness Gap: What Sarah Doesn't Know

These are the critical facts Sarah has never encountered. If she knew these, everything would change.

❌
Bills can be inaccurate or inflated — She assumes if the hospital sent it, it must be correct
❌
She has legal rights — No Surprises Act protections, ability to negotiate, financial assistance programs exist
❌
Cash prices are often cheaper than insurance — Never occurs to her to ask about cash pay options
❌
Medical billing errors are common — Studies estimate 80% of bills contain errors
❌
She can validate charges against benchmarks — Doesn't know fair prices exist and are now publicly available
❌
Hospitals have financial assistance — Many families qualify for aid but never apply
❌
The "deductible" explanation might be wrong — Claims can be miscoded or overcharged
The Core Insight

Sarah thinks the system is working as designed and she just got unlucky. She doesn't realize the system is designed to overcharge and that she has power to fight back.
